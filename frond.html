Study 006: Electric Frond

The reference is less about one clean leaf than about noisy overprint density: curved palm ribs partially swallowed by lavender static, with color shifting from cool violet/cyan at the top to magenta and orange near the lower stem.

Press S to save, R for a new seed. The sliders redraw the print, changing leaflet count, rib curvature, color-temperature, and grain load.

  • Leaflets are multi-pass Bezier strokes, not flat shapes, so their tips dissolve into noise.
  • Cool and hot pigment layers are intentionally misregistered by a few pixels.
  • Background stipple is drawn both before and after the frond to imitate scan noise and ink dust.

Process notes

I decomposed the reference into substrate, shadowed sweeping arcs, central stem, detached lower dash marks, and high-frequency grain. The sketch draws each leaflet several times: a broad translucent under-paint, a saturated core, a cyan/violet offset edge, then a final field of dots that partially buries the shape.

// Core move: build one palm leaflet as a noisy stack of Bezier strokes.
const base = frondPoint(t);
const tip = frondPoint(t + span);
for (let pass = 0; pass < 5; pass++) {
  strokeWeight(w * (1 - pass * .16));
  stroke(r, g, b, alpha * (1 - pass * .13));
  bezier(base.x, base.y, c1.x, c1.y, c2.x, c2.y, tip.x, tip.y);
}
