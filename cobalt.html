Study 003: Cobalt Eclipse Field

The reference reads like an ink test plate: two large circular bodies, a bent field of dot arcs, and scratches crossing a dark sea of stipple. This version uses cobalt as the dominant ink and bone-gold as exposed paper.

Press S to save, R for a new seed. The sliders change density, warped arc structure, scratch exposure, and ragged-edge erosion.

Process notes

I treated your reference as layered printmaking, not as vector geometry. The circles are masking events; tone is built from thousands of independent stipple marks; the arcing region is a field of repeated curves; the diagonal marks are scratches where a warm underlayer cuts through the cobalt ink.

// Core decision: each dot asks whether it lives in a disc, arc-field, or dark sea.
const tone = discTone(x, y) + arcTone(x, y) - erosionAtEdge(x, y);
if (random() < tone) drawStipple(x, y, chooseInk(tone));
